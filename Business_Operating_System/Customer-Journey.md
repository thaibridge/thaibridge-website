## Stage 01: Discovery (Source → Website)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Searching Google/WeChat/LinkedIn for "进入泰国市场" or "Thailand market entry" or "Thai warehouse" or "Thai KOL"
- Or: being referred by an existing client, partner, or industry contact
- Or: seeing ThaiBridge content on LinkedIn / WeChat

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- SEO: ranking for relevant search terms
- Content: publishing on LinkedIn / WeChat
- Network: asking existing contacts for referrals
- Website: clearly communicating who we are and what we do in the first 5 seconds

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Website content (homepage, services pages)
- LinkedIn posts
- WeChat content
- Referral requests to existing network

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Client visits website and clicks "Book a Call" or emails contact@thaibridge.asia
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 02 (Book a Call)

---

## Stage 02: Book a Call (Website → Contact)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Reading the website and deciding whether ThaiBridge is credible
- Filling in contact info or clicking email CTA
- Sending a message: who they are, what they need

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Providing clear CTA buttons on every page
- Contact page shows: Email, WeChat, LINE, Hours, Location
- AI Sales Manager: auto-acknowledges email within 1 hour

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Inbound lead logged in Client Pipeline (03_Client_Pipeline.md)
- Auto-response email template sent

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Client has contacted ThaiBridge and initial info is logged
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 03 (Discovery Meeting)

---

## Stage 03: Discovery Meeting (First Contact → Qualified)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Attending a 30–45 minute call/video meeting
- Explaining their brand, product, goals, timeline, concerns
- Asking questions about ThaiBridge's capabilities and process

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- AI Sales Manager: schedules meeting, sends calendar invite
- CEO/Account Manager: prepares meeting brief (research client background)
- Meeting agenda: listen → understand → assess fit → explain relevant services → set expectations
- Honest assessment: if we can't help, say so and refer if possible

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Meeting notes logged in CRM
- Client qualified or disqualified
- If qualified: agreed next steps (usually → Needs Analysis)

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Client is qualified (matches ideal client profile, has budget, has realistic timeline)
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 04 (Needs Analysis)
- **Exit Path:** Client not a fit → polite decline, refer if possible, log reason

---

## Stage 04: Needs Analysis (Qualified → Scoped)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Providing detailed information about their business, products, goals, constraints
- Answering follow-up questions about volume, budget, timeline, decision process
- May provide existing data, reports, or documentation

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Internal analysis: which services fit this client?
- Scope definition: what's included, what's not, timeline, resource requirements
- Risk assessment: what could go wrong, what are the blockers
- Market Research Analyst (AI): if service includes research, begin preliminary data gathering

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Internal Needs Analysis Document
- Scope of Work draft
- Pricing estimate

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Clear scope and pricing estimate ready for client presentation
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 05 (Proposal + Quotation)

---

## Stage 05: Proposal + Quotation (Scoped → Priced)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Receiving a tailored proposal document
- Reviewing scope, timeline, deliverables, and pricing
- Comparing with alternatives (if any)
- Asking clarifying questions

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- AI Sales Manager: generates proposal draft from Service Bible pricing models
- CEO reviews and customizes proposal
- Send proposal within 3 business days of needs analysis completion
- Follow up within 48 hours to answer questions

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- **Proposal Document** — scope, timeline, deliverables, team, pricing, terms
- **Quotation** — detailed pricing with assumptions and exclusions

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Client receives proposal and wants to proceed (may enter negotiation)
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 06 (Negotiation)
- **Exit Path:** Client declines → log reason, stay in touch for future opportunities

---

## Stage 06: Negotiation (Priced → Agreed)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Negotiating on scope, timeline, or pricing
- May request adjustments to deliverables or payment terms
- May involve multiple stakeholders (CEO, finance, operations)

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Negotiate in good faith — scope can flex, quality cannot
- Pricing principles (from Service Bible): we don't discount below sustainable margin
- All changes to scope documented and reflected in revised proposal
- Set clear expectations: what happens when, who does what, how we communicate

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Revised Proposal (if scope changed)
- Final pricing agreement
- Contract draft

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Both parties agree on scope, pricing, and terms
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 07 (Contract)
- **Exit Path:** Negotiation fails → log reason, leave door open

---

## Stage 07: Contract (Agreed → Signed)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Reviewing contract terms
- May have legal team review
- Signing the agreement
- Paying first invoice (if upfront payment required)

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- AI Operations Manager: generates contract from template with project-specific details
- CEO reviews contract before sending
- Send contract within 24 hours of negotiation close
- Follow up if not signed within 5 business days

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- **Signed Service Agreement**
- **First Invoice** (if applicable)
- Project added to Project Tracker (04_Project_Tracker.md)

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Contract signed by both parties, first payment received (if applicable)
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 08 (Execution)

---

## Stage 08: Execution (Signed → Delivering)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Participating in onboarding kickoff meeting
- Providing required information, access, and materials
- Reviewing weekly progress reports
- Making decisions when escalation is needed
- Paying monthly invoices (retainer clients)

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Account Manager: kickoff meeting per SOP-LE-001
- Service Leads: executing per respective SOPs (Warehouse, Creator, Matching, Research)
- Operations Manager: monitoring all workstreams, generating weekly reports per SOP-LE-002
- Weekly: progress report to client (Friday 18:00 ICT)
- Monthly: performance dashboard
- Issues: escalated and resolved per SOP-LE-003

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- **Weekly Progress Reports** (every Friday)
- **Monthly Performance Dashboard**
- Service-specific deliverables per Service Bible

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** All project deliverables completed, client signs off
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 09 (Project Complete)
- **Exit Path:** Client terminates early → per contract terms → exit interview → log learnings

---

## Stage 09: Project Complete (Delivered → Reviewed)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Reviewing final deliverables
- Providing feedback on the engagement
- Deciding: continue with retainer or end engagement
- If ending: receiving transition package (documentation, partner introductions)

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Deliver final project report
- Conduct project retrospective (internal)
- Client satisfaction survey (NPS)
- If continuing: transition to retainer
- If ending: transition per SOP-LE-004 (knowledge transfer, partner handover)

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- **Final Project Report**
- **Client NPS Score** (logged in KPI system)
- **Project Retrospective** (internal — what went well, what to improve)
- If transitioning: **Transition Package**

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition A:** Client continues with retainer → Stage 10 (Long-term Partnership)
- **Condition B:** Client ends engagement → Stage 11 (Referral Program — ask for referral)

---

## Stage 10: Long-term Partnership (Project → Retainer)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Operating in Thailand with ThaiBridge as ongoing partner
- Scaling what works, cutting what doesn't
- Quarterly strategy discussions
- May expand scope (new channels, new products, new services)

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Monthly retainer service per agreed scope
- Quarterly strategy reviews and planning
- Continuous optimization of operations
- Proactive opportunity identification (new channels, new partners, new creators)
- Annual partnership review

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- **Monthly Operations Reports**
- **Quarterly Strategy Reviews**
- **Annual Partnership Review**

### ④ Transition to Next Stage
- **Condition:** Client is happy and willing to refer
- **Next Stage:** → Stage 11 (Referral)

---

## Stage 11: Referral (Client → Growth Engine)

### ① What the Client Is Doing
- Referring ThaiBridge to other brands, partners, or industry contacts
- May provide testimonial or case study (with permission)
- May speak at events or on reference calls

### ② What ThaiBridge Is Doing
- Ask for referral (timing: after successful project delivery or quarterly review)
- Make it easy: provide referral message template, introduction email draft
- Thank and acknowledge referrer (gift, discount, or public recognition with permission)
- Track referral source in CRM

### ③ Output (Deliverable)
- Referral logged in CRM (source tracking)
- Testimonial / case study (with client permission)
- New lead enters pipeline at Stage 01

### ④ Loop Back
- Referred lead enters at **Stage 01** (Discovery)
- Cycle continues

## Exit Points & Recovery

| Stage | Exit Reason | Recovery Action |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| 03 Discovery | Not a fit | Refer if possible. Log reason. Stay connected on LinkedIn/WeChat. |
| 05 Proposal | Proposal declined | Understand why. Log for learning. Follow up in 3 months. |
| 06 Negotiation | Cannot agree | Leave door open. Check in after 3 months. |
| 08 Execution | Early termination | Contract terms apply. Exit interview. Learn and improve. |
| 09 Complete | Ends naturally | Transition support. Ask for referral. Stay in touch. |

---

## Key Principles

1. **Every stage has an owner** — no orphaned clients between stages
2. **Every stage produces a deliverable** — even if it's just meeting notes
3. **AI handles routine work** — humans handle judgment calls
4. **Speed builds trust** — respond within 4 hours, deliver within 3 business days
5. **"No" is a valid outcome** — we'd rather lose a deal than take a bad-fit client
6. **Referral is the primary growth engine** — every happy client is a distribution channel
