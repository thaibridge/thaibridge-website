### What The Client Does
- Fills website form OR sends WeChat message OR emails OR adds LINE
- Message typically says: who they are, what they sell, that they need help with Thailand
- May ask a specific question or request a call

### What ThaiBridge Does

**AI Sales Manager (automated):**
1. Receives message
2. Creates CRM record: Company Name, Contact Person, Source, Date, Message Content
3. Sends auto-response within 1 hour:
   > "Thank you for reaching out. I'm the ThaiBridge team based in Bangkok. We help Chinese brands enter the Thai market with verified local resources and real execution. I'd love to learn about your brand and goals. When would be a good time for a 30-minute call? You can also reach me on WeChat at [ID]. — [Name]"

**Human (CEO/Account Manager):**
4. Reviews CRM entry
5. Personalizes follow-up if needed
6. Prepares for discovery call (research company, check industry fit)

### Stage Output
- CRM record created (Stage: LEAD → CONTACTED)
- Auto-response sent
- Discovery call scheduled or in negotiation

### Decision Point
- **If client responds and schedules call:** → STAGE 3
- **If no response in 3 days:** AI sends polite follow-up
- **If no response after 3 follow-ups (over 2 weeks):** → LOST (with 3-month re-engagement reminder)

### Trust Signals We Send
- Response within 1 hour (speed = competence)
- Bilingual response (Chinese or English based on their message language)
- Specific, not generic ("I'm based in Bangkok" not "Thank you for your interest")
- Clear next step proposed (30-minute call, specific time)

### Timeframe
- First response: ≤ 1 hour (AI auto-response)
- Human follow-up: ≤ 4 business hours
- Call scheduling: 1–3 days

```
   │• Current│          │  feasibility│        │• Next steps │
   │  status │          │• Risk flags │        │  clarity    │
   └─────────┘          └─────────────┘        └─────────────┘
```

### ① LISTEN Phase (First 10 Minutes)

**Client talks. We listen.**

Key questions we ask:
- "Tell me about your brand and what you sell."
- "What made you interested in the Thailand market?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What's your biggest concern about entering Thailand?"

**We do NOT:**
- Pitch services yet
- Talk about ourselves
- Interrupt with solutions

### ② ASSESS Phase (Next 15 Minutes)

**We ask deeper questions. We're evaluating fit against our Ideal Client Profile.**

Checklist:
- [ ] Product category — do we have expertise here?
- [ ] Current business scale — can they afford our services?
- [ ] Timeline — is it realistic?
- [ ] Budget — have they thought about this?
- [ ] Decision-maker — are we talking to the right person?
- [ ] Red flags — unrealistic expectations, bad prior experiences, urgency without clarity

**Fit Assessment:**
- 🟢 Strong fit: matches ICP, has budget, realistic timeline
- 🟡 Possible fit: needs more information before we can say yes
- 🔴 Not a fit: wrong category, no budget, unrealistic expectations

**If NOT a fit:** We say so directly. "Based on what you've told me, I don't think ThaiBridge is the right partner for you right now. Here's why. And here's what I'd recommend instead." ← This builds more trust than a forced sale.

### ③ ADVISE Phase (Last 10 Minutes)

**We recommend which services match their needs.**

- "For where you are right now, I'd suggest starting with [Service A] because [reason]. Some brands in your category also benefit from [Service B], but that can come later."
- "Here's typically what the process looks like: [brief overview of relevant stages]."
- "The next step would be for us to put together a tailored proposal. No cost, no commitment. It takes about [timeframe]. Does that sound useful?"

### Stage Output

| Output | Format | Owner |
|--------|--------|:-----:|
| Meeting Notes | CRM record | AI Ops Manager (from call transcript) |
| Fit Assessment | Green / Yellow / Red | CEO |
| Recommended Services | List with rationale | CEO |
| Agreed Next Step | Proposal / More Info / Not a Fit | CEO |

### Decision Point
- **Go:** Client wants a proposal → STAGE 4
- **More Info:** Client needs time or more details → Follow-up with additional materials, re-engage
- **Not a Fit:** Honest decline → polite exit, offer to refer, stay connected
- **We say no:** We don't have the right capabilities → refer if possible

### Timeframe
- Call: 30–45 minutes
- Post-call summary to client: within 24 hours
- Transition to proposal: 1–3 business days

### Proposal Document Structure

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Who you are, what you need, how we can help
   - High-level timeline and investment

2. **Understanding of Your Business**
   - Restate what we learned in the discovery call
   - Shows we listened and understood

3. **Recommended Services**
   - Per service: what we'll do, how we'll do it, what you'll get
   - "Why this service" — tied to their specific situation

4. **Scope of Work**
   - Included: exactly what we'll deliver
   - Excluded: what we won't do (and why — usually "you should do this yourself" or "we recommend a specialist")
   - Assumptions: what must be true for this to work

5. **Timeline**
   - Phase-by-phase with milestones
   - Dependencies noted

6. **Investment**
   - Service fees (our retainer / project fee)
   - Estimated third-party costs (warehouse fees, creator fees, registration costs)
   - Payment schedule
   - NOTE: "All third-party costs are paid directly by you to the provider. ThaiBridge does not mark up partner costs."

7. **Why ThaiBridge**
   - Brief credibility section
   - Specific to their situation

8. **Next Steps**
   - What happens if you say yes
   - Timeline to kickoff

### Stage Output

| Output | Owner |
|--------|:-----:|
| Proposal Document (PDF) | AI generates draft → CEO reviews and customizes |
| Quotation Spreadsheet | AI generates from pricing models |

### Decision Point
- **Accepted without changes:** → STAGE 6
- **Accepted with changes:** → Revised proposal → back to client
- **Declined:** → LOST (log reason, 3-month re-engagement)

### Timeframe
- Proposal generation: 1–2 business days
- Client review: 1–7 days
- Revisions (if needed): 1–2 business days per round

### Contract Essentials

- **Service description:** What we will do
- **Timeline:** Start date, milestones, completion target
- **Fees & payment:** Amount, schedule, method
- **Third-party costs:** Clarified as client-direct
- **Minimum commitment:** 3 months (for retainer services)
- **Termination:** Notice period, early termination terms
- **Confidentiality:** Both parties
- **Governing law:** Thailand

### Kickoff Meeting Agenda

1. Introductions: who's who on both sides
2. Review scope and timeline (confirm nothing changed)
3. Communication plan: how often, which channels, who talks to whom
4. Client's responsibilities: what we need from them and when
5. First week priorities
6. Q&A

6. Recommendations for next phase
7. Appendix: all deliverables, reports, data files

### Client Sign-Off

- Client reviews final deliverables
- NPS survey sent: "On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend ThaiBridge to another brand entering Thailand?"
- Sign-off document (simple: "I confirm receipt and acceptance of all project deliverables")

### Retainer Service Model

Client pays monthly retainer. ThaiBridge provides ongoing:
- Operations management
- Weekly reporting
- Monthly performance review
- Quarterly strategy sessions
- Issue resolution
- New opportunity identification

**Retainer can flex up or down** based on client needs. If they're scaling, we scale with them. If they're in maintenance mode, we reduce scope.

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

Every 90 days:

1. **Performance Review:** What happened last quarter vs. plan
2. **Market Update:** What's changed in the Thai market relevant to them
3. **Opportunity Scan:** New channels, creators, partners, products
4. **Strategy Adjustment:** What to do differently next quarter
5. **Resource Check:** Do we need more/less ThaiBridge involvement?

### Cross-Selling

As we work with a client, we identify opportunities to add services:

| Current Service | Natural Cross-Sell |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Warehouse only | "Your fulfillment is solid. Ready to build consumer awareness? We can connect you with creators." |
| Creator campaigns only | "Your content is working. Ready to find a local distribution partner?" |
| Research only | "The data says go. Ready to execute? We can handle the setup." |

Cross-selling works because: (1) we've already proven ourselves on one service, (2) the client already trusts us, (3) it's genuinely better for them — one partner instead of multiple vendors.
