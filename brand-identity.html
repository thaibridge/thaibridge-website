Design Philosophy

The Bridge as Architecture

A bridge is not decoration. It is infrastructure — designed to carry weight, withstand pressure, and last for generations. The ThaiBridge mark is built on this principle: two solid pillars connected by an arch — China on one side, Thailand on the other, ThaiBridge as the span between them.

Why It Works

The mark communicates three things simultaneously: stability (the pillars are grounded, architectural), connection (the arch bridges two distinct points), and passage (the negative space forms a gateway — an invitation to move from one side to the other).

The Name

"ThaiBridge" — the word itself is the strategy. "Thai" anchors us geographically and culturally. "Bridge" defines our function. No abstraction needed. Set in Inter at 700 weight with tight letter-spacing, the wordmark reads like a company that knows what it does and doesn't need to explain itself.

The Golden Arch

The gold element (#C8A951) in the arch is the only color accent in an otherwise monochromatic system. It represents the ThaiBridge difference — the human judgment, the local relationships, the trust that AI alone cannot provide. One color. One promise.

5. Clear Space & Minimum Size

The logo requires breathing room to maintain its presence. Clear space is measured using the icon's pillar width (W).

Clear space = 1x pillar width (W) on all sides. Minimum logo width: 120px (digital), 40mm (print). Icon-only minimum: 24px (digital).

Usage Rules

Gold (#C8A951) — used ONLY for the bridge arch element and the "Bridge" portion of the wordmark. Never used for body text. Never used as a background.

Pillars — always white (#EDF1F7) on dark backgrounds. Navy (#060B14) on light backgrounds. Never gold.

Background — Deep navy (#060B14) for digital. White/off-white for print. The logo never sits on a gray or colored background — only navy or white.

7. Monochrome Variants

For single-color applications: watermark, embossing, engraving, fax documents.

Monochrome: all-white on navy · all-navy on white. No gold accent in monochrome variant.

8. Typography

The wordmark uses Inter Bold (700) with letter-spacing tightened to -0.6px. Inter was chosen for its neutrality, clarity, and excellent rendering at all sizes — from 12px UI labels to hero headlines.

Design Summary

The Mark

Two pillars. One arch. The simplest possible expression of a bridge — architectural, permanent, load-bearing. No decoration. No metaphor you need to decode.

The Colors

Gold + Deep Navy + White. Three colors total. The gold is used sparingly — only on the arch and the "Bridge" portion of the wordmark — making it the most meaningful color in the system.

The Type

Inter Bold at 700 weight. A modern grotesk that renders cleanly at every size. The wordmark says exactly what the company does — "Thai" + "Bridge" — with no abstraction needed.
