Icon Design · 5 Concepts

ThaiBridge Symbol

Each concept solves the same problem: communicate trust, connection, and permanence — across two cultures, in one mark that works at 16x16 pixels and on an office door.

01

The Gateway

ARCHITECTURAL · PERMANENT · FORMAL

Two grounded pillars. One spanning arch. The negative space between them forms a portal — an invitation from one side to the other. The arch is gold because the connection is the value ThaiBridge provides. This is the most literal translation of "bridge" into icon form, executed with the restraint of corporate architecture rather than a travel logo.

Scalability Test
Strengths
  • Instantly reads as a bridge — zero cognitive load
  • Architecture metaphor = permanence and stability
  • Distinctive at all sizes, including favicon
  • Works perfectly as monochrome emboss
    • Risks
  • Most literal — may feel too obvious to some
  • Must guard against looking like a construction logo
  • Rounded pillars + arch can read as "gate" not "bridge" at tiny sizes
    • 02

    The Interlock

    ABSTRACT · KINETIC · MODERN

    Two rounded rectangular forms overlap and interlock — one gold, one white — creating a shared space where both elements are equally strong and neither dominates. The interlocking geometry suggests a handshake, a partnership contract, two puzzle pieces that complete each other. This concept leans most toward Stripe-style abstraction: it rewards the second look.

    Scalability Test
    Strengths
  • Abstract — rewards repeated viewing
  • Interlocking = partnership, mutual dependence
  • Two distinct colors = China + Thailand clearly represented
  • Works in monochrome by varying stroke opacity
    • Risks
  • Does not directly say "bridge" on first glance
  • At 16px, overlapping area becomes mushy
  • May read as Venn diagram or "two companies merging"
    • 03

    The Rise

    DYNAMIC · ASPIRATIONAL · GROWTH

    A single continuous line starts from the left (origin), rises upward in a smooth arc (growth trajectory), and descends to the right (destination). The gold line thickness increases through the ascent — the partnership itself is where value is created. This concept leans toward the Notion/Linear aesthetic: single-line minimalism that implies progress and momentum.

    Scalability Test
    Strengths
  • The "rising bridge" = growth narrative embedded in the mark
  • Two dots = two markets. The arc = the path between them
  • Single continuous line is extremely elegant and modern
  • Works beautifully as a brand motif (can animate the line drawing)
    • Risks
  • May read as "stock chart" or "Wi-Fi signal" if not carefully executed
  • At 16px the two dots disappear or merge
  • Less architectural — may feel too "tech startup" for a cross-border ops company
    • 04

    The Pillar

    SOLID · INSTITUTIONAL · TIMELESS

    Two thick, grounded vertical blocks. Between them — not an arch, but a horizontal beam set at a deliberate angle, suggesting a bridge deck under load. This is the most architectural, most institutional, most Apple-esque concept. It says "we have been here, we will be here." The angled beam is the only dynamic element — it says "but we move things forward."

    Scalability Test
    Strengths
  • Visceral sense of structure and permanence — most "trust-like"
  • Angled beam = forward motion without losing solidity
  • Extremely simple — 3 shapes. Reads clearly at 16px
  • Monochrome version is equally strong
    • Risks
  • Angled horizontal could read as "declining" or "warning"
  • At very small sizes, the angle needs to steepen to remain visible
  • May feel too rigid — lacks warmth
    • 05

    The Golden Thread

    ELEGANT · HUMAN · DISTINCTIVE

    Two solid vertical forms. A single, unbroken gold thread weaves between them — starting from outside the left pillar, passing through both, and emerging on the right. The thread represents the continuous flow of trust, communication, and value that ThaiBridge enables. Unlike a static arch, the thread implies ongoing movement. This concept is the most Airbnb-meets-Stripe: warm geometry that feels both mathematical and human.

    Scalability Test
    Strengths
  • Most distinctive — the weaving thread is unique and memorable
  • "Thread of trust" is a powerful narrative for a partnership company
  • Soft S-curves add humanity to an otherwise geometric system
  • The thread passing THROUGH the pillars (not over) says "we are inside both markets"
    • Risks
  • More complex than the other concepts — 3 shapes + 1 path + 2 dots
  • At 16px the thread becomes very thin and the curve may vanish
  • Risk of reading as "cable management" or "data flow" if executed poorly
    • Recommendation

    Concept 01: The Gateway

    For ThaiBridge specifically — a company whose name is its function, whose primary audience is business owners evaluating risk, whose value proposition is stability and permanence — the literal bridge icon is the strongest choice.

    Reasoning: Abstract marks (Concepts 02, 03, 05) require the viewer to learn what the symbol means. For a consumer brand like Nike or Apple, that investment pays off. For a B2B trust-based business where the customer needs to feel confidence in the first 3 seconds, clarity beats cleverness. The Gateway says "bridge" instantly — and that's exactly what ThaiBridge sells.

    Refinements to make before production:

    • Slightly widen the gap between pillars to improve 16px rendering
    • Increase arch thickness by 0.5px for stronger presence at small sizes
    • Round the pillar tops slightly more (rx=3.5 instead of rx=3) for a friendlier feel
    • Remove the highlight line from the standard version — keep it only for the "premium" variant
    • The arch curve should start slightly below the pillar tops, not at them — this creates a subtle sense of load-bearing

    If we want to be bolder: Concept 05 (The Golden Thread) is the strongest alternative. It maintains the pillar structure while adding distinctive character — the weaving thread that passes through both sides. This rewards the second look and creates a richer brand story. But it demands more from the viewer, which is a tradeoff we should only make intentionally.
