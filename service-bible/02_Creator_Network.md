# 02 — Creator & KOL Network

## 1. Mission

To connect Chinese brands with vetted Thai creators who deliver real engagement — not vanity metrics — and to manage the entire creator relationship so the brand gets authentic local content without the cross-border friction.

---

## 2. Customer

**Primary:** Chinese consumer brands that need to build awareness and trust with Thai consumers through localized content created by Thai voices.

**Profile:**
- Consumer brand (beauty, fashion, electronics, F&B, lifestyle)
- Product ready for Thai market or launching soon
- Monthly content marketing budget: $3K–$30K
- Has tried creator partnerships through agencies or platforms and been disappointed by fake followers, poor communication, or content that didn't convert
- Needs both UGC (volume, authenticity) and KOL (reach, credibility)

**NOT a fit if:**
- No Thai-language product packaging or localization → needs Market Entry (Service 04) first
- Monthly budget < $1,000 → cannot deliver meaningful results
- Product is highly regulated (pharmaceuticals, financial services) → TODO

---

## 3. Customer Pain Points

1. **Cannot distinguish real from fake influencers.** Follower counts are inflated. Engagement is fabricated. Past campaign data is unavailable or unreliable. Every bad creator wastes budget and launch window.
2. **Cross-cultural creator management is high-friction.** Briefs get lost in translation. Content doesn't match brand guidelines. Timelines slip. No one on the ground to visit a creator's studio or resolve issues in real time.
3. **Agencies mark up creator fees 30–50%.** The brand pays a premium for access that should be transparent.
4. **No long-term creator relationships.** Agencies treat every campaign as transactional. Creators have no loyalty to the brand. Each campaign starts from zero.

## 5. Service Workflow

### Phase 1: Discovery & Strategy (Week 1)
- Brand immersion: product, positioning, target audience, visual identity, past content performance
- Category audit: what content is working for competitors in Thailand?
- Define: UGC/KOL mix, platform focus (TikTok, IG, YouTube), campaign KPIs
- Output: **Content Strategy Document**

### Phase 2: Creator Sourcing & Vetting (Week 2)
- Shortlist 8–15 creators from vetted network matching the brief
- Verify each: audience demographics, engagement rate (not follower count), content quality, brand safety, past collaboration references
- Present **Creator Portfolio** — photos, stats, sample content, pricing per creator
- Client selects final lineup

### Phase 3: Briefing & Content Planning (Week 2–3)
- Create **Bilingual Creative Brief** (Thai for creators, Chinese/English for client approval)
- **Content Calendar**: posting dates, platforms, formats, key messages
- Product seeding logistics: ship samples, track delivery
- Pre-production review: scripts, shot lists, brand guideline check

### Phase 4: Content Production (Week 3–5)
- Monitor creator progress against calendar
- Review drafts against brief before posting
- Coordinate posting schedule across creators
- Capture all content assets for brand's own channels

### Phase 5: Reporting & Optimization (Week 5–6)
- Compile performance data: views, engagement, clicks, conversion estimates
- Bilingual **Campaign Performance Report**: what worked, what didn't, recommendations
- Creator performance scoring — top performers added to brand's preferred roster

## 8. Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Creator posts underperform | Medium | Diversify roster; never bet on 1–2 creators; performance-based scoring for future selection |
| Creator fails to post on schedule | Medium | Content calendar with buffer days; backup creator pool; contract delivery clauses |
| Brand unhappy with creative quality | Low | Pre-production review; detailed bilingual brief; reference content examples |
| Influencer scandal / brand safety issue | Low | Background check before engagement; brand safety clause in contract |

| Creator fees higher than budgeted | Medium | Lock pricing during vetting phase; present range, not fixed quote, before client commits |

## 10. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

### SOP-CR-001: Creator Vetting
1. Receive creator application or identify candidate through network
2. Request: audience demographic breakdown (age, gender, location), 3-month engagement data, 3 most recent brand collaborations with performance results
3. Verify data through third-party analytics tool (TODO: select tool)
4. Conduct video call or in-person meeting — assess professionalism, communication, brand alignment
5. For mid-tier and above: studio visit. Verify equipment, team, production capability.
6. Background check: social media history scan, brand safety flag check
7. Rate creator: A (premium, proven), B (solid, reliable), C (emerging, potential), D (do not use)
8. Add to vetted network database with full profile and rating

### SOP-CR-002: Campaign Briefing
1. Receive approved Content Strategy and Creator Portfolio from client
2. Create Bilingual Creative Brief:
   - Brand background (Chinese + Thai)
   - Product information (Chinese + Thai)
   - Key messages (Chinese + Thai)
   - Visual guidelines (visual examples — universal language)
   - Do's and Don'ts
   - Deliverables per creator (format, quantity, deadline)
3. Send brief to creators in Thai
4. Schedule 15-min kickoff call with each creator (Thai language)
5. Confirm understanding — ask creator to repeat brief in their own words
6. Share confirmed Content Calendar with client

### SOP-CR-003: Content Review
1. Creator submits draft 48 hours before scheduled post date
2. Check against: brand guidelines, key messages, format requirements
3. Flag issues: off-brand visuals, incorrect product claims, missing disclosures
4. Provide feedback to creator in Thai (≤ 1 round of revisions)
5. If revision satisfactory → approve for posting
6. If revision unsatisfactory → escalate to Account Manager → discuss with client
7. Confirm post is live within 2 hours of scheduled time
8. Capture screenshot + link for performance tracking

### SOP-CR-004: Campaign Reporting
1. Wait 7 days after final post for engagement data to stabilize
2. Pull data: per-creator, per-platform (views, likes, comments, shares, saves, clicks)
3. Calculate engagement rates, CPM, estimated reach
4. Compare against campaign KPIs and category benchmarks
5. Compile bilingual report (Chinese/English summary, raw data in appendix)
6. Creator performance scoring: rank all creators on this campaign
7. Present report to client within 10 days of campaign end
8. Debrief call: what to repeat, what to change, next campaign planning
