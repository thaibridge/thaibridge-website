# 03 — Business Matching

## 1. Mission

To connect Chinese companies with verified, trustworthy local partners in Thailand — distributors, suppliers, service providers, and retail channels — and to stay involved through every negotiation and the first year of the partnership to ensure it actually works.

---

## 2. Customer

**Primary:** Chinese companies that need a local partner in Thailand but don't know how to find one that won't disappoint them.

**Profile:**
- Manufacturer seeking Thai distribution partners
- Brand seeking local supplier or co-manufacturer
- Company seeking retail placement (convenience stores, department stores, specialty retail)
- Service business seeking local JV partner
- Has budget and timeline for partnership development
- Has been burned by "introductions" that went nowhere

**NOT a fit if:**
- No existing business → wants ThaiBridge to create demand from zero
- Requirement is full-time local hire → this is recruitment, not business matching
- Requirement is purely legal/registration with no ongoing partnership → refer to corporate service provider

---

## 3. Customer Pain Points

1. **No way to verify potential partners from abroad.** Online directories and personal networks produce low-quality leads. Claims about capabilities, reputation, and financial stability cannot be verified without local presence.
2. **Cross-cultural negotiation breaks deals.** Different expectations about pricing, exclusivity, payment terms, and contract enforcement cause deals to fail — often after significant time has been invested.
3. **No one to escalate to when problems arise.** After a partnership is formed, issues come up. Without a local intermediary, small problems become deal-breaking conflicts.
4. **The cost of a bad partnership dwarfs the cost of finding one.** A bad distributor can waste 12–18 months and damage brand reputation in the market permanently.

## 5. Service Workflow

### Phase 1: Requirements Definition (Week 1–2)
- Client workshop: define ideal partner profile
- Key criteria: capabilities, scale, location, financial health, cultural fit, exclusivity
- Success definition: what does "good" look like at 12 months?
- Non-negotiables: what disqualifies a candidate immediately?
- Output: **Ideal Partner Profile Document**

### Phase 2: Partner Sourcing (Week 2–4)
- Search existing vetted network
- Direct outreach to candidates not yet in network
- Industry association and trade body referrals
- Initial bilingual screening call with 8–12 candidates
- Shortlist 3–5 for deep evaluation
- Output: **Longlist Summary**

### Phase 3: Due Diligence (Week 4–6)
- Site visit to each shortlisted partner
- Reference checks: 3+ existing clients or business partners
- Financial health assessment (public records, credit check where available)
- Capability audit: visit facility, meet team, review operations
- Output: **Partner Evaluation Report** — 3–5 candidates, scored on all criteria

### Phase 4: Introduction & Negotiation (Week 6–8)
- Facilitated bilingual introduction meeting
- Client selects preferred partner
- Bilingual negotiation support: pricing, terms, exclusivity, performance commitments
- Contract review: flag terms deviating from Thai or Chinese business norms
- Output: **Contract Summary** with risks and cultural notes

### Phase 5: Partnership Launch (Week 8–12)
- First project / order / collaboration kickoff
- Weekly check-ins for first 8 weeks
- Issue resolution as needed
- 90-day partnership health check
- Output: **90-Day Health Check Report**

### Phase 6: Ongoing Stewardship (Month 4–12, optional retainer)
- Monthly check-ins
- Performance monitoring against agreed KPIs
- Escalation handling
- Annual partnership review and optimization

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Partnership fails despite vetting | Medium | Due diligence is thorough but not infallible; stewardship catches issues early |
| Client and partner cut ThaiBridge out post-introduction | Medium | Contract defines ongoing role; stewardship retainer incentivizes continued value |
| Partner misrepresents capabilities | Low | Site visit + reference checks + capability audit before recommendation |
| Cultural misunderstanding derails negotiation | Medium | Bilingual facilitation in every meeting; cultural brief provided to both sides |

| Partner has hidden financial problems | Low | Financial review catches most; stewardship monitoring detects early warning signs |

## 10. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

### SOP-BM-001: Partner Screening Call
1. Prepare: review candidate's public information, prepare screening questions based on client's Ideal Partner Profile
2. Conduct 30-minute bilingual call (Thai with candidate, notes in Chinese/English for client)
3. Assess: communication quality, professionalism, apparent capability, alignment with client needs
4. Rate: Green (advance to shortlist), Yellow (potential but needs more verification), Red (do not advance)
5. Document screening notes in Longlist Summary within 24 hours

### SOP-BM-002: Partner Site Visit
1. Schedule visit with partner (≥ 1 week notice)
2. Pre-visit preparation: review all claims made by partner, prepare verification checklist
3. On-site agenda:
   - Facility tour (60 min)
   - Team introduction and key personnel interview (30 min)
   - Operations demonstration (30 min)
   - Q&A session (30 min)
4. Documentation: photos of facility, team, operations; notes on observations vs. claims
5. Reference calls (separate from site visit): contact 3+ references, verify specific claims
6. Complete Partner Evaluation Report within 5 business days of visit

### SOP-BM-003: Bilingual Negotiation Facilitation
1. Pre-negotiation: prepare both sides. Share cultural expectations brief with client. Share Thai business norms context.
2. In-meeting role: facilitate communication, not dominate it. Translate accurately — don't soften or harden either side's position.
3. Flag cultural gaps in real time: "In Chinese business culture, this term means X. In Thai business culture, it's typically interpreted as Y."
4. Post-meeting: written summary in both languages within 24 hours. Confirm both sides have same understanding.
5. Contract review: flag any terms that deviate significantly from market norms in either country.

### SOP-BM-004: Partnership Health Check (90-Day)
1. Schedule separate calls with client and partner
2. Assess against original success criteria from Ideal Partner Profile
3. Identify: what's working, what's not, what needs to change
4. Facilitate joint discussion if issues exist
5. Document Health Check Report with Green/Yellow/Red status and action items
6. If Red: activate escalation protocol → propose remediation plan or transition plan
