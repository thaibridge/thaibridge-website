# 01 — Overseas Warehouse & Fulfillment

## 1. Mission

To give Chinese brands a trustworthy, personally-vetted warehouse partner in Thailand — and to manage the daily fulfillment operations so the client never has to worry about where their products are, whether orders are shipping on time, or whether their warehouse partner is being honest with them.

---

## 2. Customer

**Primary:** Chinese cross-border e-commerce sellers and D2C brands selling physical products to Thai consumers.

**Profile:**
- Annual Thai market revenue: $50K–$2M (early-stage to growth-stage)
- Currently ships from China; experiencing 7–15 day delivery times
- Losing sales due to slow shipping, inability to offer COD, or high return rates
- Has tried self-managing warehouse relationships and been burned
- Sells on Shopee, Lazada, and/or TikTok Shop Thailand

**NOT a fit if:**
- No existing demand in Thailand → needs Market Research (Service 04) first
- Product requires cold chain / temperature-controlled logistics → TODO
- Volume exceeds $10M/year → needs enterprise 3PL referral

---

## 3. Customer Pain Points

1. **Cannot find a trustworthy warehouse.** Directories list operators who overpromise. No way to verify claims from China. Language barrier makes negotiation impossible.
2. **No local escalation.** When the warehouse makes mistakes — damaged inventory, missed SLAs, hidden fees — there is no one local to resolve it. The client is 2,000 km away and doesn't speak Thai.
3. **No operational infrastructure.** Even after finding a warehouse, the client lacks systems for daily fulfillment tracking, inventory reconciliation, and returns processing.
4. **Unpredictable costs.** Without local market knowledge, clients overpay for storage, pick-pack, and shipping because they don't know market rates.

## 5. Service Workflow

### Phase 1: Discovery (Week 1–2)
- Client intake: product type, SKU count, dimensions, monthly volume, storage requirements, delivery SLA targets, budget range
- Define success criteria with client
- Output: **Requirements Document**

### Phase 2: Warehouse Matching (Week 2–3)
- Shortlist 3–5 warehouses from our vetted network
- Conduct site visits — photos, video walkthrough for client
- Verify: insurance, WMS capability, staffing, existing client references
- Present **Warehouse Comparison Matrix** with scored recommendation
- Client selects preferred partner

### Phase 3: Negotiation & Contract (Week 3–4)
- Bilingual contract negotiation
- Key terms: pricing tiers, SLA commitments, liability clauses, termination conditions
- Client signs directly with warehouse — ThaiBridge is NOT a reseller
- Output: **Contract Summary** with key terms translated and risks flagged

### Phase 4: Onboarding (Week 4–6)
- Coordinate first inbound shipment from China
- Set up inventory management system / WMS access for client
- Establish daily/weekly reporting cadence
- Conduct test fulfillment — 5–10 orders, verify end-to-end
- Output: **Go-Live Checklist**

### Phase 5: Ongoing Operations (Week 6+)
- Daily: SLA compliance monitoring
- Weekly: bilingual inventory reconciliation report
- Monthly: performance review, cost optimization recommendations
- As-needed: issue resolution, escalation handling
- Output: **Monthly Operations Report**

## 10. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

### SOP-WH-001: Warehouse Site Visit
1. Schedule visit with warehouse manager (≥ 3 business days notice)
2. Pre-visit checklist: confirm address, contact person, language preference
3. On-site inspection checklist:
   - [ ] Facility condition (cleanliness, organization, security)
   - [ ] Racking and storage systems
   - [ ] WMS demonstration (live, not screenshots)
   - [ ] Staff interview (ask about daily workflow, not just manager)
   - [ ] Existing client reference check (≥ 3 clients)
   - [ ] Insurance certificate verification
   - [ ] Fire safety and security systems
4. Photo documentation: entrance, storage area, packing station, shipping area, WMS screen
5. Post-visit report: complete within 48 hours → Comparison Matrix update

### SOP-WH-002: Client Onboarding
1. Receive signed warehouse contract from client → file in project folder
2. Create shared folder: client name / warehouse / onboarding
3. Coordinate first shipment: confirm date, carrier, BOL, SKU list
4. WMS access setup: create client login, verify language settings, test access
5. Test fulfillment: place 5 test orders through client's e-commerce platform
6. Verify: order received in WMS → picked → packed → tracking number generated → delivered
7. Go-Live confirmation: client signs off Go-Live Checklist
8. Schedule first weekly check-in (within 7 days of go-live)

### SOP-WH-003: Weekly Inventory Reconciliation
1. Pull inventory report from WMS every Monday 9:00 AM ICT
2. Cross-reference with client's e-commerce platform inventory data
3. Flag discrepancies > 2% variance → investigate immediately
4. Compile bilingual report (Thai data, Chinese/English summary)
5. Send to client by Monday 6:00 PM ICT
6. File report in client project folder

### SOP-WH-004: Issue Escalation
1. Issue reported (client, warehouse, or internal monitoring)
2. Acknowledge within 2 hours → confirm receipt to reporter
3. Investigate: contact warehouse in Thai, gather evidence, determine root cause
4. Resolution proposal within 8 hours
5. Client approval (if needed) → implement
6. Resolution confirmed within 24 hours of initial report
7. Post-mortem: document issue and resolution → share with client → update risk register
