# 04 — Market Research & Entry Strategy

## 1. Mission

To give Chinese brands reliable, on-the-ground intelligence about their specific category in Thailand — gathered through primary research, not desktop reports — so they can decide whether and how to enter the market with confidence.

---

## 2. Customer

**Primary:** Chinese companies at the pre-entry or early-exploration stage. They know Thailand is attractive but don't know if their specific product will work — at what price, through which channel, against which competitors.

**Profile:**
- Brand or manufacturer with successful domestic business in China
- Considering Thailand as first international market or next ASEAN market
- Has NOT yet committed significant resources to Thailand
- Needs objective intelligence, not desktop research or ChatGPT summaries
- Willing to invest in research before committing to operations

**NOT a fit if:**
- Already operating in Thailand → needs Local Execution (Service 05) or optimization
- Wants "guarantee" of success before entering → research reduces risk, doesn't eliminate it
- Timeline < 2 weeks → meaningful primary research takes time
- Budget < $3,000 → cannot deliver quality at lower price point

---

## 3. Customer Pain Points

1. **Cannot get reliable category-specific information without being on the ground.** Online research in English/Chinese misses 80% of the picture. Thai consumer data, competitor activity, and channel dynamics are only visible in Thai language and in physical retail. Google cannot tell you what shelf your competitor occupies at 7-Eleven.
2. **Cannot interpret data without local context.** A price point that works in China may be premium or budget in Thailand. A distribution channel that drives volume in China may not exist. Brands need translation of business context, not just language.
3. **Syndicated reports are too generic.** "Thailand E-Commerce Market Report 2025" tells you about the market but nothing about your specific category, your specific competitors, or your specific consumers.
4. **Consultants charge $20K+ for this.** Good market entry strategy from top-tier firms is priced for Fortune 500 budgets.

## 5. Service Workflow

### Phase 1: Scoping (Week 1)
- Client briefing: product, target consumer, hypotheses to test, decisions to inform
- Define research scope: what questions must this answer for the client to decide?
- Research plan: methodology, timeline, budget
- Output: **Research Plan**

### Phase 2: Field Research — Market Landscape (Week 1–3)
- Competitive audit: all direct/indirect competitors, pricing, positioning, distribution
- Channel mapping: where is this category sold? (online platforms, modern trade, traditional trade, specialty retail)
- Pricing analysis: price bands, promotion patterns, estimated margin structure
- Regulatory review: import requirements, labeling, certifications, restrictions
- Output: **Market Landscape Report** (internal draft)

### Phase 3: Field Research — Consumer (Week 2–4)
- Consumer interviews: 10–20 target consumers. Purchase behavior, brand perception, willingness to pay, unmet needs
- Retail observation: in-store behavior, shelf placement, point-of-sale activity, shopper demographics
- Digital audit: social media conversation analysis, search volume trends, competitor digital presence
- Output: **Consumer Insights Report** (internal draft)

### Phase 4: Analysis & Synthesis (Week 4–5)
- Connect market landscape data to consumer insights
- Opportunity sizing: addressable market estimate, realistic Year 1 projection
- Go-to-market recommendation: pricing strategy, channel priority, launch sequence, marketing approach
- Risk assessment: key barriers, competitive response scenarios, regulatory hurdles
- Output: **Draft Market Entry Recommendation**

### Phase 5: Delivery (Week 5–6)
- Final bilingual research report (Chinese or English per client preference)
- Executive presentation: key findings and recommendation
- Decision workshop: go/no-go discussion, concrete next steps if "go"
- Optional: warm handoff to Service 05 (Local Execution) if client decides to enter

## 8. Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Research recommends "don't enter" → client unhappy they paid for a "no" | Low | Set expectation during scoping: honest answer is the valuable answer. "Don't enter" saves far more than research cost |
| Research data becomes outdated quickly | Medium | Timestamp all data; note shelf life of findings; offer monitoring retainer |
| Client disagrees with recommendation | Low | All recommendations backed by primary data; transparent about confidence levels and assumptions |
| Consumer interview sample not representative | Medium | Recruit across multiple channels and demographics; transparent about sample size and limitations |
| Competitor data incomplete (private companies) | Medium | Acknowledge gaps; use observable data (pricing, shelf presence, digital activity) as proxy where financials unavailable |

## 10. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

### SOP-MR-001: Research Scoping
1. Schedule 60-minute scoping call with client decision-maker (not just point of contact)
2. Key questions to answer:
   - What decision does this research inform? (go/no-go, pricing, channel, positioning)
   - What would make you say "no" to Thailand?
   - What would make you say "yes"?
   - What do you already believe about this market? (test these as hypotheses)
   - What's your budget range and timeline?
3. Draft Research Plan: methodology, key questions, timeline, budget
4. Client approval before any field work begins
5. Scope change process: any addition to scope → revised timeline and fee estimate → client approval

### SOP-MR-002: Competitive Audit
1. Define competitor universe: direct (same product category, same price band), indirect (substitutes, adjacent categories)
2. For each competitor, collect:
   - Product: SKUs in Thai market, packaging (Thai language?), sizing
   - Pricing: shelf price, promotional price, estimated margin
   - Distribution: where sold (platforms, retail chains, channels)
   - Marketing: social media presence, creator partnerships, ad activity
   - Consumer perception: online reviews, social sentiment (Thai language)
3. Sources: physical store visits, e-commerce platform search, social media, industry contacts
4. Document with photos, screenshots, links — every claim must be verifiable
5. Competitive positioning map: price vs. quality perception, mass vs. premium

### SOP-MR-003: Consumer Interview
1. Recruit 10–20 participants matching target consumer profile
2. Recruitment channels: social media, consumer panels, in-store intercept
3. Interview format: 30–45 min, semi-structured. Thai language.
4. Core question areas:
   - Current purchase behavior in this category
   - Brand awareness and perception (competitors)
   - Price sensitivity and willingness to pay
   - Unmet needs / frustrations with current options
   - Reaction to client's product concept (if applicable)
5. Compensate participants (market rate — TODO: standard amount)
6. Record and transcribe (Thai). Analysis in English/Chinese.
7. Include participant demographic summary in report

### SOP-MR-004: Report Delivery
1. Draft report → internal review (Account Manager + Partnership Director)
2. Client delivery meeting: 60 min presentation + 30 min Q&A
3. Report structure:
   - Executive Summary (2 pages max — the answer first)
   - Market Landscape (competitive, channel, pricing, regulatory)
   - Consumer Insights (what consumers said, what it means)
   - Recommendation (pricing, channel, launch sequence, risks)
   - Appendix (methodology, participant profiles, raw data)
4. Decision workshop (separate meeting if needed): facilitate go/no-go discussion
5. If "go": present transition path to Service 05 (Local Execution)
6. Archive: all research files in client project folder
