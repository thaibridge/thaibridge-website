# 05 — Local Execution & Operations

## 1. Mission

To be the local operating team that Chinese brands need in Thailand — handling company setup, warehouse onboarding, creator campaigns, partner negotiations, and daily operations — so the client can focus on their product while we make everything else happen on the ground.

---

## 2. Customer

**Primary:** Chinese brands that have decided to enter Thailand and need a local team to execute — not a consultant to recommend, but operators who do the work.

**Profile:**
- Has validated Thailand market opportunity (through ThaiBridge Service 04 or internal research)
- Has committed budget for Thailand market entry
- Needs 3–12 months of operational support to establish local presence
- May eventually hire own Thailand team — ThaiBridge bridges the gap
- Values speed and reliability over lowest cost

**NOT a fit if:**
- Wants to hire a full-time country manager immediately → we can refer recruiters
- No product-market fit validation → needs Market Research (Service 04) first
- Budget cannot support 3-month minimum operational commitment

---

## 3. Customer Pain Points

1. **Setting up in a foreign country is a full-time job.** But sending an expat is expensive ($80K–$150K/year), slow to recruit (2–3 months), and risky (single point of failure). The founder cannot relocate to Bangkok for 6 months.
2. **Cross-border operations involve dozens of interdependent workstreams.** Company registration, bank account, warehouse, e-commerce platform onboarding, creator partnerships, logistics contracts. Done sequentially from China: 12–18 months. Done in parallel with a local team: 3–6 months.
3. **Every workstream requires Thai language and local knowledge.** Each one has different vendors, different processes, different pitfalls. Learning as you go costs time and money.
4. **There is no single point of accountability.** When the warehouse has an issue, the creator misses a deadline, and the bank needs another document — all in the same week — who handles it? Without a local team, the founder handles everything, and everything becomes a crisis.

## 5. Service Workflow

### Month 1: Foundation
- Company registration: Thai entity setup, tax ID, VAT registration
- Bank account: corporate bank account opening
- Office/workspace: identify and secure (if needed)
- Legal & compliance: import licenses, product registration, trademark protection
- E-commerce platform onboarding: Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop seller accounts
- Milestone: **Company operational in Thailand**

### Month 1–2: Infrastructure
- Warehouse: finalize partner (Service 01), sign contract, first inbound shipment from China
- Logistics: last-mile delivery contract, COD setup
- Payment: local payment gateway integration
- Customer service: Thai-language support setup (in-house or partner)
- Milestone: **First orders shipping to Thai customers**

### Month 2–3: Market Activation
- Creator partnerships (Service 02): first campaign briefed and in production
- Distribution: first retail or channel partner meetings (Service 03)
- Marketing: localized content, social media accounts, initial ad campaigns
- Pricing: final Thai market pricing across all channels
- Milestone: **Brand live and visible in Thai market**

### Month 3–6: Operations & Optimization
- Weekly: sales review, inventory check, creator content calendar, issue resolution
- Monthly: P&L review, performance against plan, optimization recommendations
- Quarterly: strategy review, next-quarter planning, budget adjustment
- Milestone: **Stable, growing operations**

### Month 6–12: Scaling & Transition
- Double down on what's working; cut or pivot what's not
- If client hiring own team: knowledge transfer, process documentation, warm handover
- If client continuing: expand scope (new channels, products, regions)
- Milestone: **Self-sustaining or scaled operations**

## 8. Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Client expectations exceed retainer scope | Medium | Detailed scope document signed at start; scope change process defined; monthly reviews catch misalignment |
| Third-party delays (government, banks) | Medium | Build buffer into timeline; manage expectations about uncontrollable factors |
| Client's product doesn't sell in Thailand | Low | Market research should precede execution; flag risk if client skipped research |
| Client wants early termination (< 3 months) | Low | Minimum commitment clause; but if relationship isn't working, clean exit > forced continuation |
| Team stretched across too many clients | Medium | Cap active clients based on team capacity; quality over quantity; hire before scaling |
| Client becomes dependent, can't transition to own team | Medium | Build transition plan from Day 1; all processes documented for eventual handover |

## 10. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

### SOP-LE-001: Client Onboarding
1. Signed service agreement + first month retainer received
2. Kickoff meeting: client team + ThaiBridge Account Manager + relevant service leads
3. Create shared project folder: client name / local-execution
4. Complete Client Intake Form:
   - Company details, product info, target timeline, key contacts
   - Priority workstreams (what must happen first?)
   - Risk factors (regulatory complexity, timeline pressure, budget constraints)
   - Communication preferences (language, frequency, channels)
5. Build 90-day operational plan with milestones, owners, deadlines
6. Client approval on plan
7. Weekly cadence: Monday status update, Friday progress report
8. First weekly report delivered within 7 days of kickoff

### SOP-LE-002: Weekly Client Reporting
1. Compile inputs from all active workstreams by Friday 12:00 ICT
2. Report structure:
   - Executive Summary (3 bullets — what happened this week)
   - Workstream Status (Green/Yellow/Red per workstream)
   - Key Metrics (sales, inventory, creator posts, partner meetings)
   - Issues & Blockers (what needs client attention)
   - Next Week Priorities
3. Internal review by Account Manager before sending
4. Send to client by Friday 18:00 ICT
5. File in project folder
6. Client feedback incorporated into Monday planning

### SOP-LE-003: Issue Escalation
1. Issue identified (by any team member, client, or partner)
2. Log in issue tracker with: date, description, severity (P1/P2/P3), owner
3. P1 (business-critical): acknowledge ≤ 2 hours, resolution plan ≤ 8 hours, resolved ≤ 24 hours
4. P2 (important, not urgent): resolution plan ≤ 24 hours, resolved ≤ 72 hours
5. P3 (nice to fix): addressed in weekly planning
6. Client notified of all P1 and P2 issues — no surprises
7. Post-resolution: document root cause and prevention for future

### SOP-LE-004: Client Transition (to internal team)
1. Client notifies intent to transition ≥ 8 weeks before target date
2. Transition plan: which functions, to whom, by when
3. Process documentation: all active workflows documented in SOP format
4. Partner introductions: all active partners introduced to new team
5. Knowledge transfer sessions: 2–4 weeks of parallel running
6. Phased handover: function by function, not all at once
7. Final 30 days: ThaiBridge in advisory role, new team operating
8. Transition complete: client sign-off; ThaiBridge available for ad-hoc support (billable)
