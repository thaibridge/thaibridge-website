Case Study 01

Chinese Beauty Brand Enters the Thai Market

How a mid-size Chinese skincare brand went from zero Thai presence to operational in 8 weeks — with ThaiBridge as their local execution partner.

The Client

Brand A

  • Chinese skincare brand with strong domestic sales and a growing international ambition
  • Had validated product-market fit in China (annual revenue in the tens of millions RMB)
  • Products: premium sheet masks, serums, and sun care — categories with proven demand in Thailand
  • Had NO prior international market experience
  • Goal: launch in Thailand within 3 months with local warehousing, e-commerce presence, and creator marketing
The Challenge

What Brand A Was Facing

1

No local knowledge: The brand had never operated outside China. They didn't know Thai consumer preferences, pricing norms, or distribution channels.

2

Warehouse search was failing: The founder had contacted 6 warehouses through online directories. Three never responded. Two sent quotes in Thai that couldn't be verified. One seemed promising but had no track record with Chinese brands.

3

Creator market was opaque: The brand knew they needed Thai creators for authentic content, but every agency quote was 30-50% above what they'd budgeted — and none could verify creator engagement data.

4

Timeline pressure: They had committed to a launch date for a Thai distributor who was waiting for local fulfillment capability before signing.

5

Language barrier: No one on the team spoke Thai. Every interaction with a Thai partner required a translator, and nuance was getting lost.

ThaiBridge's Approach

What We Did

Four workstreams, one team, 8 weeks.

Market Research

Week 1–2

Conducted competitive audit of the Thai beauty category: 12 competitor brands mapped by price, positioning, and distribution channel Interviewed 15 Thai beauty consumers about their skincare routines, brand preferences, and willingness to try a new Chinese brand
Delivered: Market Entry Recommendation — pricing strategy, channel prioritization, and launch sequence

Warehouse Setup

Week 2–4

Visited 4 warehouses in Bangkok and Samut Prakan Negotiated bilingual contract with storage and fulfillment SLA terms Set up WMS access for the client in Chinese First shipment from China arrived and was checked in within 3 weeks of project start
Delivered: Operational warehouse with 98% same-day fulfillment SLA

Creator Campaign

Week 3–6

Sourced and vetted 12 Thai beauty creators across TikTok and Instagram Verified: real engagement rates (3.2% avg across the roster — above the 1.5% category benchmark), authentic audiences (≥ 85% Thai followers), past brand collaboration performance Created bilingual creative brief emphasizing the brand's "clean beauty" positioning Managed content production: 24 posts across 12 creators in 3 weeks
Delivered: Campaign with 450K+ combined views, 4.1% avg engagement rate, and 3 creators re-booked for the next campaign

Business Matching

Week 4–8

Identified 5 potential Thai distribution partners through our network Vetted each through site visits and reference checks Facilitated bilingual negotiations with the top candidate Brand A signed a distribution agreement in Week 8
Delivered: Signed distribution partnership with an established Thai beauty distributor

What We Learned

Three Takeaways

Speed matters more than perfection. The brand's willingness to make fast decisions (warehouse selection in 1 week, not 1 month) was the single biggest factor in the 8-week timeline.
Lesson 1
Creator authenticity beats production value. The highest-engagement posts were unscripted 'morning routine' content, not polished product reviews.
Lesson 2
A vetted partner changes everything. The distribution deal closed in 2 weeks because we could say 'we visited their warehouse, checked their references, and have worked with them before' — instead of 'we found them online.'
Lesson 3
