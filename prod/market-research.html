Why You Need This

Most Thailand market research isn't
worth the PDF it's printed on.

Chinese brands are flying blind because the research they can access was never designed for them.

01

Can't Get Reliable Category Data Without Being on the Ground

Online research in English or Chinese gives you macro statistics — GDP, population, internet penetration. It tells you nothing about what is actually happening on shelf, what consumers are buying, or how competitors are pricing in your specific category.

02

Can't Interpret Data Without Local Context

A 15% market share for brand X means nothing unless you understand the retail structure, regional consumption patterns, and cultural drivers behind that number. Without local interpreters, you misread the data — and make costly strategy errors.

03

Syndicated Reports Are Too Generic

Euromonitor, Nielsen, and Mintel reports cover broad categories at a 30,000-foot level. They do not tell you which SKU formats sell at 7-Eleven vs. Lotus's, what price point triggers trial in your subcategory, or which TikTok content themes are driving conversion for your competitors.

04

Big Consulting Firms Charge $20K+

McKinsey, BCG, and Bain will produce a beautiful deck — but their Thailand entry studies start at $20,000 and go up from there. The research is often desk-based, supplemented by a few expert interviews conducted in English. You pay a premium for the brand, not the depth of local insight.

How We Work

From research question to actionable
recommendation in five steps.

A structured research process that prioritizes what you actually need to make a decision.

1

Scoping

We define the research questions that matter for your decision: which categories, which consumer segments, which retail channels, which competitors, which price points. We identify what you already know and what gaps the research must fill. Output: a Research Plan with methodology, timeline, and specific deliverables. We scope to your budget and timeline — not to a template.

2

Field Research: Market Landscape

Our team visits retail channels across Bangkok (and other cities as needed): modern trade (Tesco Lotus, Big C, Tops), convenience (7-Eleven, FamilyMart), specialty retailers, and e-commerce platforms (Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop). We audit: which brands are on shelf, SKU formats and sizes, shelf placement, pricing at full price and promotion, packaging and claims, and POS materials. We photograph everything. Output: Competitive Audit and Channel Map.

3

Field Research: Consumer

We conduct in-depth interviews with target consumers — recruited by demographic, category usage, and purchase behavior. Interviews are conducted in Thai, in-home or in-context (e.g., at point of purchase). We explore the full purchase journey, category attitudes, brand perceptions, price sensitivity, unmet needs, and openness to foreign brands. We also conduct digital ethnography — analyzing Thai social media conversations, TikTok content themes, and consumer reviews in your category.

4

Analysis & Synthesis

We synthesize all primary and secondary data into a coherent narrative. We identify patterns across retail, consumer, and digital data. We benchmark your product proposition against the competitive landscape. We model pricing scenarios. We assess regulatory requirements and barriers. We pressure-test our findings with industry experts. The result: insights linked directly to implications for your business.

5

Delivery & Decision Workshop

You receive a comprehensive written report in Chinese and an executive summary in English. We then run a live Decision Workshop (in person or via video) — presenting findings, answering questions, and facilitating a structured discussion around our recommendation. If the recommendation is "go," we present a phased entry roadmap with timelines, investment estimates, and next steps. If "no-go," we explain exactly why — and what would need to change for the answer to flip. No hedging. No "it depends." A specific, defensible recommendation.

FAQ

Common questions about our
market research service.

Straight answers from our research team in Bangkok.

How long does the research take?+
A typical research engagement runs 4-8 weeks from scoping to final presentation. Weeks 1-2: scoping and research design. Weeks 2-5: field research (market landscape and consumer interviews run in parallel). Weeks 5-7: analysis and synthesis. Week 8: report delivery and Decision Workshop. The timeline depends on scope — a single-category, single-city study can be completed in 4 weeks. A multi-category, multi-city study may require 10-12 weeks. We will give you a precise timeline during scoping.
What if you recommend we DON'T enter Thailand?+
Then we will have saved you far more than the cost of the research. We have delivered "no-go" recommendations before — when the competitive intensity is too high, the price point does not work, the regulatory barriers are prohibitive, or the category is simply too small to justify the investment. A "no-go" recommendation is not a failed engagement; it is a successful one. Our reputation depends on honesty, not on convincing every client to enter. The research fee is the same either way, so we have no incentive to bias the recommendation toward "go."
How many consumers do you interview?+
A standard engagement includes 15-25 in-depth consumer interviews, plus 3-5 expert interviews (distributors, category buyers, industry veterans). For quantitative validation, we can add an online survey (n=200-500) if needed, though we typically recommend this only when the qualitative phase surfaces a hypothesis that needs statistical confirmation. Our philosophy is that 20 well-recruited, well-interviewed consumers yield more actionable insight than 500 survey respondents answering multiple-choice questions drafted without local context. We design the methodology to match your decision — not to maximize sample size for its own sake.
Can you research B2B categories, not just consumer?+
Yes. For B2B categories (industrial supplies, automotive components, packaging, machinery, etc.), our methodology shifts from retail audits and consumer interviews to distributor interviews, trade association research, industrial park visits, and direct outreach to potential B2B buyers. We also analyze import data, tariff structures, and regulatory requirements specific to B2B categories. The fundamental principle is the same: primary research conducted in Thai, by people on the ground, producing specific recommendations. B2B research typically takes longer (8-12 weeks) due to longer recruitment timelines for niche expert interviews.
What happens after the research is delivered?+
If the recommendation is "go," we can transition directly into execution — warehouse setup, creator campaigns, business matching, or a full local execution retainer. Because we conducted the research, there is no knowledge loss or re-briefing required. The research becomes the foundation of your operational plan. If you prefer to execute independently, the report is designed to be standalone — with enough detail that your internal team or another partner can act on it. There is no obligation to continue with us. That said, most clients do — because the research builds trust in our capabilities and the transition from insight to execution is seamless.
