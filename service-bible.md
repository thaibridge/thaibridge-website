## Service 1: Overseas Warehouse

### 1.1 Who Is the Customer?

Chinese cross-border e-commerce sellers, D2C brands, and manufacturers who sell physical products to Thai consumers and need local storage, fulfillment, and last-mile delivery.

**Typical profile:**
- Annual Thai market revenue: $50K–$2M (early-stage to growth-stage)
- Ships from China currently; experiencing 7–15 day delivery times
- Losing sales due to long shipping, high return rates, or inability to offer COD (cash on delivery)
- Has tried self-managing warehouse relationships and been burned by unreliable operators
- May already sell on Shopee, Lazada, or TikTok Shop Thailand

**Not a fit if:**
- The brand has no existing demand in Thailand (they need Market Research first)
- The product requires cold chain / temperature-controlled logistics (we don't cover this yet)
- The brand is doing $10M+/year in Thailand and needs enterprise 3PL (we can refer them)

### 1.2 What Problem Do They Have?

**Primary:** They cannot find a trustworthy warehouse partner in Thailand. Directories are full of operators who overpromise and underdeliver. Language barriers make negotiation impossible. Site visits from China are expensive and infrequent. When problems happen — damaged inventory, missed SLAs, hidden fees — there is no one local to escalate to.

**Secondary:** Even if they find a warehouse, they lack the operational infrastructure to manage daily fulfillment, inventory reconciliation, and returns processing. They need someone who can speak Thai to the warehouse team and Chinese to them.

### 1.3 Why Is ThaiBridge Different?

| Dimension | Typical Broker | ThaiBridge |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Warehouse selection | Forwards from a paid directory | Personally visits every warehouse before recommending |
| Language | Client must handle Thai communication | Bilingual team manages all warehouse communication |
| Accountability | Introduction made, broker disappears | We stay involved through setup and daily operations |
| Vetting | Self-reported capabilities | On-site inspection, reference checks, test shipment |
| Problem resolution | Client is on their own | We escalate in Thai, report in Chinese, resolve locally |

### 1.4 Step-by-Step Workflow

```
WEEK 1–2        DISCOVERY
                 • Client intake: product type, volume, storage requirements,
                   delivery SLA expectations, budget range
                 • Define success criteria: delivery time target, coverage area,
                   special handling needs

WEEK 2–3        WAREHOUSE MATCHING
                 • Shortlist 3–5 warehouses from our vetted network
                 • Conduct site visits (photos, video walkthrough for client)
                 • Verify: insurance, WMS capability, staffing levels,
                   existing client references
                 • Present comparison matrix to client with recommendation

WEEK 3–4        NEGOTIATION & CONTRACT
                 • Facilitate bilingual contract negotiation
                 • Key terms: pricing tiers, SLA commitments, liability clauses,
                   termination conditions
                 • Client signs directly with warehouse (ThaiBridge is NOT a
                   reseller — we don't mark up warehouse fees)

WEEK 4–6        ONBOARDING
                 • Coordinate first inbound shipment from China
                 • Set up inventory management system / WMS access for client
                 • Establish daily/weekly reporting cadence
                 • Conduct test fulfillment: 5–10 orders, verify end-to-end

WEEK 6+         ONGOING OPERATIONS
                 • Daily: monitor fulfillment SLA compliance
                 • Weekly: inventory reconciliation report to client (bilingual)
                 • Monthly: performance review, cost optimization recommendations
                 • As needed: issue resolution, escalation handling
```

### 1.5 Deliverables

| Phase | Deliverable |
|-------|-------------|
| Discovery | Requirements Document — storage type, volume forecast, SLA targets |
| Matching | Warehouse Comparison Matrix — 3–5 options scored on 8 dimensions |
| Negotiation | Contract Summary — key terms translated, risks flagged |

| Ongoing | Monthly Operations Report — SLA performance, cost analysis, recommendations |

  - Standard (1 warehouse, 1 location, < 5 SKUs): $2,000–$3,000
  - Complex (multiple warehouses, multi-location, > 20 SKUs): $4,000–$6,000

**Option B: Monthly Retainer**

**Note:** We never take commission from warehouse partners. This is non-negotiable — it would create a conflict of interest in our recommendations.

## Service 2: Creator & KOL Network

### 2.1 Who Is the Customer?

Chinese consumer brands that need to build awareness and trust with Thai consumers through localized content. These brands understand that Thai consumers trust Thai voices — but they don't know how to find, vet, and manage Thai creators effectively.

**Typical profile:**
- Consumer brand (beauty, fashion, electronics, food & beverage, lifestyle)
- Already has product ready for Thai market (or about to launch)
- Monthly content marketing budget: $3K–$30K
- Has tried working with creators through agencies or platforms and been disappointed by fake followers, poor communication, or content that didn't convert
- Needs both UGC (volume, authenticity) and KOL (reach, credibility)

**Not a fit if:**
- The brand has no Thai-language product packaging or localization (they need Market Entry first)
- The budget is under $1K/month (cannot deliver meaningful results at this scale)
- The product category is highly regulated (pharmaceuticals, financial services)

### 2.2 What Problem Do They Have?

**Primary:** They cannot distinguish between real and fake influencers in Thailand. Follower counts are inflated. Engagement rates are fabricated. Past campaign performance data is unavailable or unreliable. Every bad creator partnership wastes both budget and the critical launch window.

**Secondary:** Managing creator relationships across language and cultural barriers is extremely high-friction. Briefs get lost in translation. Content doesn't match brand guidelines. Timelines slip. There's no one on the ground to visit a creator's studio, verify their setup, or resolve issues in real time.

### 2.3 Why Is ThaiBridge Different?

| Dimension | Typical MCN / Agency | ThaiBridge |
|-----------|---------------------|------------|
| Creator selection | Database with self-reported stats | Personally audited — verified audience data, past campaign performance, studio visits |
| Briefing | Google Translate or broken English | Bilingual brief in Thai, brand guidelines translated with cultural context |
| Reporting | Vanity metrics (impressions, likes) | Engagement rates, CPM comparison, conversion data where trackable |
| Relationship | Transactional — campaign ends, relationship ends | We build long-term creator rosters for each brand — same creators across campaigns builds cumulative trust |
| Pricing | Agency marks up creator fees 30–50% | Creator fees passed through at cost; ThaiBridge charges service fee separately |

### 2.4 Step-by-Step Workflow

```
WEEK 1          DISCOVERY & STRATEGY
                 • Brand immersion: product, positioning, target audience,
                   visual identity, previous content performance
                 • Category audit: what content is working for competitors?
                 • Define content strategy: UGC mix, KOL mix, platform focus
                   (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube), campaign KPIs

WEEK 2          CREATOR SOURCING & VETTING

                   brand safety check, past collaboration references
                 • Present creator portfolio — photos, stats, sample content, pricing
                 • Client selects final creator lineup

WEEK 2–3        BRIEFING & CONTENT PLANNING
                 • Create bilingual creative brief (Thai for creators,
                   Chinese for brand approval)
                 • Content calendar: posting dates, platform, format, key messages
                 • Product seeding logistics: ship samples to creators,
                   track delivery
                 • Pre-production: script review, shot list approval

WEEK 3–5        CONTENT PRODUCTION
                 • Monitor creator content creation progress
                 • Review drafts against brief before posting
                 • Coordinate posting schedule across multiple creators
                 • Capture all content assets for brand's own channels

WEEK 5–6        REPORTING & OPTIMIZATION
                 • Compile performance data: views, engagement, link clicks,
                   conversion estimates
                 • Bilingual campaign report: what worked, what didn't,
                   recommendations for next campaign

                   brand's preferred roster

## Service 3: Business Matching

### 3.1 Who Is the Customer?

Chinese companies that need to find trustworthy local partners in Thailand — distributors, suppliers, service providers, manufacturing partners, or retail channels. They know they need a local partner but don't know how to find one that won't disappoint them.

**Typical profile:**
- Manufacturer seeking Thai distribution partners
- Brand seeking local supplier or co-manufacturer
- Company seeking retail placement (convenience stores, department stores, specialty retail)
- Service business seeking local JV partner
- Has budget and timeline for partnership development
- Has been burned by "introductions" that went nowhere

**Not a fit if:**
- The company has no existing business and wants ThaiBridge to create demand from zero
- The requirement is for a full-time local hire (this is recruitment, not our service)
- The requirement is purely for legal/company registration with no ongoing partnership need

### 3.2 What Problem Do They Have?

**Primary:** Finding a local partner through personal networks or online directories produces low-quality leads. Without local presence, there's no way to verify a potential partner's claims — their capabilities, their reputation, their financial stability. The cost of a bad partnership (wasted time, lost revenue, damaged reputation) far exceeds the cost of finding one.

**Secondary:** Even when a promising partner is identified, cross-cultural negotiation is extremely difficult. Both sides have different expectations about pricing, exclusivity, payment terms, communication cadence, and contract enforcement. Without a bilingual intermediary who understands both business cultures, deals fall apart during negotiation — or worse, after signing.

### 3.3 Why Is ThaiBridge Different?

| Dimension | Typical Business Broker | ThaiBridge |
|-----------|------------------------|------------|
| Partner sourcing | Database search based on self-reported category | Referral network + direct outreach + in-person verification |
| Vetting | Reference check (optional, often skipped) | Mandatory: site visit, reference check, financial review, capability audit |
| Negotiation | Client handles alone | Bilingual facilitation in every meeting — we translate culture, not just words |
| Post-deal involvement | Broker disappears after commission | We stay involved for 6–12 months to ensure partnership stabilizes |
| Conflict of interest | Commission from partner side creates bias | Client-paid only — no partner-side payments, no referral fees |

### 3.4 Step-by-Step Workflow

```
WEEK 1–2        REQUIREMENTS DEFINITION

WEEK 4–6        DUE DILIGENCE

                 • Present Partner Evaluation Report to client

WEEK 6–8        INTRODUCTION & NEGOTIATION
                 • Facilitated introduction meeting (bilingual)
                 • Client selects preferred partner
                 • Bilingual negotiation support: pricing, terms, exclusivity,

WEEK 8–12       PARTNERSHIP LAUNCH

## Service 4: Market Research & Entry Strategy

### 4.1 Who Is the Customer?

Chinese companies at the pre-entry or early-exploration stage. They know Thailand is an attractive market but don't know if their specific product will work — at what price, through which channel, against which competitors. They need data to decide whether and how to enter.

**Typical profile:**
- Brand or manufacturer with successful domestic business in China
- Considering Thailand as first international market
- Has NOT yet committed resources to Thailand entry
- Needs objective, on-the-ground intelligence — not desktop research
- Willing to invest in research before committing to operations

**Not a fit if:**
- The brand is already operating in Thailand and needs optimization (they need Local Execution)
- The brand wants a "guarantee" of success before entering (research reduces risk, doesn't eliminate it)
- The timeline is under 2 weeks (meaningful primary research takes time)

### 4.2 What Problem Do They Have?

**Primary:** They cannot get reliable, specific information about their category in Thailand without being on the ground. Online research in English/Chinese misses 80% of the picture — most Thai consumer data, competitor activity, and channel dynamics are only visible in Thai language and in physical retail environments. Google and ChatGPT cannot tell you what shelf your competitor occupies at 7-Eleven or what price a similar product sells for at the Chatuchak weekend market.

**Secondary:** Even when they have data, they don't know how to interpret it for a market they've never operated in. A price point that works in China may be premium in Thailand — or vice versa. A distribution channel that drives volume in China may not exist in Thailand. They need translation not just of language, but of business context.

### 4.3 Why Is ThaiBridge Different?

| Dimension | Typical Research Firm | ThaiBridge |
|-----------|----------------------|------------|
| Data source | Desktop research, syndicated reports | Primary research: store visits, consumer interviews, pricing audits, channel mapping |
| Language | English-only reports | Bilingual: field research in Thai, report in Chinese or English |
| Actionability | Market overview — interesting but not decision-useful | Specific go/no-go recommendation with pricing, channel, and launch strategy |
| Category depth | Generalist across many categories | Focused on categories relevant to Chinese brands entering Thailand |
| Post-research | Report delivered, engagement ends | Research naturally transitions to execution services if client decides to enter |

### 4.4 Step-by-Step Workflow

```
WEEK 1          SCOPING
                 • Client briefing: product category, target consumer,
                   hypotheses to test, key decisions to inform
                 • Define research scope: what questions must this research
                   answer for the client to make a decision?
                 • Research plan: methodology, timeline, budget

WEEK 1–3        FIELD RESEARCH (Phase 1: Market Landscape)
                 • Competitive audit: identify all direct and indirect
                   competitors, their pricing, positioning, distribution
                 • Channel mapping: where is this category sold?
                   (online platforms, modern trade, traditional trade, specialty)
                 • Pricing analysis: price bands, promotions, margin structure
                 • Regulatory review: import requirements, labeling, certifications

WEEK 2–4        FIELD RESEARCH (Phase 2: Consumer)
                 • Consumer interviews: 10–20 target consumers,
                   purchase behavior, brand perception, willingness to pay
                 • Retail observation: in-store behavior, shelf placement,
                   point-of-sale activity
                 • Digital audit: social media conversation analysis,
                   search volume trends, competitor digital presence

WEEK 4–5        ANALYSIS & SYNTHESIS
                 • Data synthesis: connect market landscape to consumer insights
                 • Opportunity sizing: addressable market, realistic Year 1 targets
                 • Go-to-market recommendation: pricing strategy, channel priority,
                   launch sequence, marketing approach
                 • Risk assessment: key barriers, competitive responses, regulatory hurdles

WEEK 5–6        DELIVERY
                 • Bilingual research report (Chinese or English per client preference)
                 • Executive presentation: key findings and recommendation
                 • Decision workshop: go/no-go discussion, next steps if "go"
                 • Optional: transition to entry planning (Service 5: Local Execution)
```

### 4.5 Deliverables

| Phase | Deliverable |
|-------|-------------|
| Scoping | Research Plan — methodology, timeline, key questions |
| Landscape | Competitive Audit + Channel Map + Pricing Analysis + Regulatory Overview |
| Consumer | Consumer Insights Report — interviews, behaviors, willingness to pay |
| Synthesis | Market Entry Recommendation — pricing, channel, launch strategy, risk assessment |
| Delivery | Executive Presentation + Full Research Report (bilingual) |

### 4.6 Pricing Model (Draft)

**Market Assessment (light)**
- $3,000–$5,000
- 2–3 weeks, focused on a specific question (e.g., "is there demand for our product at this price point?")
- Includes: competitive audit, pricing analysis, 5–8 consumer interviews

**Full Market Entry Study (standard)**
- $8,000–$15,000
- 5–6 weeks, comprehensive
- Includes: full competitive landscape, 15–20 consumer interviews, channel mapping, regulatory review, go-to-market recommendation

**Ongoing Market Monitoring (optional retainer)**

### 4.7 Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|

| Client disagrees with recommendation | Low | All recommendations backed by primary data; transparent about confidence levels and assumptions |
| Consumer interview sample not representative | Medium | Recruit across multiple channels; transparent about sample limitations in report |
| Competitor data is incomplete (private companies) | Medium | Acknowledge gaps; use pricing and channel presence as proxy for market share |

## Service 5: Local Execution & Operations

### 5.1 Who Is the Customer?

Chinese brands that have decided to enter Thailand and need a local operating team to make it happen. They have product, budget, and commitment — but they don't have people on the ground in Thailand to execute the day-to-day work of setting up and running operations.

**Typical profile:**
- Has completed market research (internally or through ThaiBridge Service 4)
- Has made a go decision and allocated budget for Thailand entry
- Needs 3–12 months of operational support to establish local presence
- May eventually hire their own Thailand team — ThaiBridge bridges the gap until then
- Values speed and reliability over lowest cost

**Not a fit if:**
- The brand wants to hire a full-time country manager immediately (we can help recruit but this is a different service)
- The brand has no product-market fit validation in Thailand
- The brand's budget cannot support a 3-month minimum operational commitment

### 5.2 What Problem Do They Have?

**Primary:** Setting up operations in a foreign country is a full-time job — but sending an expat is expensive, slow, and risky. The founder or executive team cannot relocate to Bangkok for 6 months. They need someone local who can execute with the authority and accountability of an internal team member, but without the cost and commitment of a full-time hire.

**Secondary:** Cross-border operations involve dozens of interdependent tasks — company registration, bank account opening, warehouse setup, e-commerce platform onboarding, creator partnerships, logistics contracts. Each task requires Thai language capability and local knowledge. Doing them sequentially from China takes 12–18 months. Doing them in parallel with a local team takes 3–6 months.

### 5.3 Why Is ThaiBridge Different?

| Time to start | 2–3 months to recruit | 1–2 weeks | 1 week |

| Flexibility | Hard to scale down | Hard to change scope mid-project | Monthly retainer — scale up or down as needed |

## Cross-Service Principles

These principles apply to every service ThaiBridge delivers:

1. **We never take commission from partners we recommend.**
   Our revenue comes from clients. Period. This eliminates the conflict of interest that destroys trust in our industry.

2. **Every partner is personally vetted before introduction.**
   No exceptions. If someone on our team hasn't visited their facility, met their team, and checked their references, they are not in our network.

3. **We report in the client's language, execute in Thai.**
   The client should never feel the language barrier. That's what they pay us for.

4. **We say no to work we cannot do well.**
   If a client's needs don't match our capabilities, we tell them — and if possible, point them to someone who can help.

5. **We stay until it works.**
   We don't hand off and disappear. Every engagement includes ongoing involvement until the client confirms the solution is stable.

6. **We are measured by results, not activity.**
   Warehouses live. Creators posting. Partners contracted. Revenue growing. Not meetings held. Not slides delivered.
