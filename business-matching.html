Why You Need This

Finding the right Thai business partner
from abroad is a minefield.

The wrong partner can destroy your Thailand entry. Here are the real risks Chinese brands face.

01

No Way to Verify Partners from Abroad

LinkedIn profiles, trade show meetings, and WeChat introductions tell you nothing about a Thai company's actual reputation, financial health, or operational capability. You are making a multi-year commitment based on a 60-minute meeting and a polished deck.

02

Cross-Cultural Negotiation Breaks Deals

Thai business negotiation follows different norms — indirect communication, relationship-first pacing, and face-saving conventions. Chinese brands that push for fast, direct terms often offend partners or misunderstand what was actually agreed. Deals fall apart before they start.

03

No One to Escalate to When Problems Arise

When your distributor misses targets, your supplier ships defective product, or your JV partner is not contributing as agreed — who do you call? Without a local escalation partner, disputes fester. Resentment builds. The partnership dies slowly and expensively.

04

The Cost of a Bad Partnership Is Enormous

A failed distribution deal wastes 12-18 months of market opportunity. A bad supplier relationship can destroy product quality and brand reputation. A broken JV can involve legal costs, asset disputes, and regulatory complications. Recovery is measured in years and millions of baht.

Partner Verification & Due Diligence

DIY
Attend trade shows, search online directories, ask your network for introductions. You cannot verify claims about revenue, capabilities, or references. Due diligence is limited to what the partner voluntarily shares — and what Google Translate can decode from Thai-language documents.
Agency
Maintain a database of "partners" who pay to be listed. Due diligence is limited to checking that the company exists and has a website. References are supplied by the partner. The agency's incentive is closing a match, not ensuring a good fit.
ThaiBridge
Mandatory site visits to every partner. We verify business registration, financial standing, facility condition, reference clients (contacted independently), and reputation in the industry. We produce a detailed Partner Evaluation Report — including risks, red flags, and our candid recommendation. If a partner does not meet our standard, they never reach your desk.

Negotiation & Deal Structure

DIY
Negotiate in English across cultural gaps. You may concede on terms that are standard in Thailand without knowing it. Or you may demand terms that are culturally offensive and walk away from a good partner over a misunderstanding.
Agency
Arrange the introduction, sit in on one meeting, then step back. The actual negotiation is between you and a partner you barely know. The agency collects their fee regardless of whether the deal terms are fair or sustainable.
ThaiBridge
We facilitate every negotiation — bilingually, in person. We know what terms are market-standard in Thailand and what is negotiable. We ensure both sides understand exactly what they are committing to. We flag terms that are unusual, risky, or unfavorable — before you sign. When cultural friction arises, we de-escalate in real time.

Post-Deal Stewardship

DIY
Once the contract is signed, you are on your own. If the partner underperforms, you have no leverage except legal action — which is slow, expensive, and conducted in a foreign legal system you do not understand.
Agency
The agency's job ends at contract signing. Post-deal issues are not their problem. If the partnership fails 3 months in, the agency will happily find you a new partner — for another fee. They are incentivized by churn, not longevity.
ThaiBridge
We stay actively involved for 6-12 months post-deal. We attend quarterly business reviews, track KPIs against the partnership agreement, mediate disputes, and manage course corrections. If the partnership is working, we gradually step back. If it is not, we diagnose the issue and drive resolution — or begin transition planning. Our success metric is partnership longevity, not match volume.

Incentive Alignment

DIY
Your incentive is clear: find a great partner. The challenge is that you lack the local knowledge, language, and network to evaluate whether a partner is truly great — or just great at presenting to foreign companies.
Agency
Agencies often take commissions from both sides — a fee from you and a "success fee" from the Thai partner. This means they are incentivized to match you with the partner who pays the highest commission, not the partner who best fits your business.
ThaiBridge
Zero partner-side commissions. Ever. Our fee is paid entirely by you, on a fixed retainer basis. We have no financial incentive to favor one partner over another. Our only incentive is finding you a partner who delivers long-term results — because that is what earns us your continued business.
How We Work

From your requirements to a live
partnership in five steps.

A rigorous process designed to eliminate the risk of a bad match before you commit.

1

Requirements Definition

We document exactly what you need from a Thai partner: category expertise, geographic coverage, channel relationships (modern trade, traditional trade, e-commerce), financial expectations, exclusivity requirements, and timeline. We pressure-test your assumptions — are you looking for the right type of partner for your stage? Output: an Ideal Partner Profile that defines what "good" looks like.

2

Partner Sourcing

We source 10-20 potential partners through our local network, industry associations, trade data, and direct outreach — all conducted in Thai. We screen each candidate against your Ideal Partner Profile and produce a Longlist Summary with our initial assessment of fit, capability, and potential red flags. You select 5-8 partners for detailed due diligence.

3

Due Diligence

For each shortlisted partner, we conduct: site visit to their office/facility, business registration verification, financial health assessment (where available), reference calls with 3-5 of their existing business partners (not references they supply — we find our own), and reputation check within their industry. Output: a Partner Evaluation Report ranking each candidate with specific strengths, weaknesses, and risks.

4

Introduction & Negotiation

We arrange in-person meetings in Bangkok (or via video if preferred). We attend every meeting — facilitating in both Thai and Chinese/English, bridging cultural norms, and ensuring both sides communicate clearly. We manage the negotiation process: term sheet, LOI, due diligence from the partner's side, and final contract. We translate all key documents and flag any terms that deviate from market standards.

5

Partnership Launch & Stewardship

Post-signing, we stay embedded for 6-12 months: facilitating the first 90 days of operations, attending QBRs, tracking KPIs against the partnership agreement, mediating any disputes, and managing course corrections. We deliver a 90-Day Health Check and ongoing monthly stewardship reports. If the partnership is thriving, we transition to a lighter-touch monitoring model. If it is struggling, we diagnose and fix — or manage an orderly transition.

FAQ

Common questions about our
business matching service.

Straight answers from our matching team in Bangkok.

How long does it take to find a partner?+
The full process typically takes 8-16 weeks from requirements definition to a signed agreement, depending on partner availability and negotiation complexity. Sourcing and due diligence takes 4-6 weeks. Introduction, negotiation, and contracting takes another 4-8 weeks. Highly specialized partner requirements (e.g., a pharmaceutical distributor with cold-chain capability in Northern Thailand) may take longer. We will give you a realistic timeline during the scoping phase — and we will not rush a match just to close faster.
What if the partnership doesn't work out?+
This is exactly why our 6-12 month stewardship period exists. If the partnership is underperforming, we diagnose the root cause — is it a capability issue, a commitment issue, or a mismatch in expectations? We mediate a corrective action plan with clear milestones. If the partner cannot or will not improve, we manage the transition — including identifying replacement partners and ensuring a clean handover. Our stewardship fee covers this work. We do not charge a new sourcing fee for a replacement within the first 12 months if the failure is due to partner underperformance verified by our KPI tracking.
Do you work on commission from either side?+
No. We charge a fixed retainer to you and accept zero commissions, referral fees, or success fees from any partner we introduce. This is fundamental to our model. We disclose this policy to every partner we engage — so they know our loyalty is to you. If a partner offers us a commission, we decline and disclose the offer to you. This alignment means you can trust that every recommendation is based on fit, not financial incentive.
Can you help with JV structuring and legal setup?+
We facilitate the commercial aspects of JV formation — partner identification, due diligence, negotiation of commercial terms, and post-formation stewardship. For legal structuring (shareholder agreements, FBA compliance, BOI applications), we work alongside your legal counsel or can recommend trusted Thai corporate law firms from our network. We do not provide legal advice directly, but we ensure the commercial deal terms are correctly reflected in legal documentation and that both sides understand their obligations. Think of us as your commercial representative — not your attorney.
What industries do you cover?+
Our core coverage includes consumer goods (FMCG, beauty, personal care, supplements, food and beverage), consumer electronics, home and lifestyle products, automotive parts and accessories, and industrial supplies. We have particular depth in categories where Chinese brands have a strong product advantage. If your industry is outside these areas, tell us — we will assess whether our network covers your category and give you a candid answer. We will not take on an engagement where we cannot add value through our existing network and expertise.
